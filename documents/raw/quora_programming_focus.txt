Title: Does the UC Merced CSE Major Focus on Programming?

Source Type: Quora Discussion

URL: https://www.quora.com/Does-the-major-of-computer-science-and-engineering-have-a-focus-on-programming-at-all-I-am-interested-in-programming-and-want-to-go-to-UC-Merced

Document Category: Program Structure and Programming Focus

Original Question:

A prospective student interested in programming asked whether the Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) major at UC Merced has a strong focus on programming.

Responses:

Contributor: Sahil Hingorani
Background:

B.S. in Computer Science, UC Santa Barbara

Opinion:

The CSE major has a strong focus on programming.
The curriculum emphasizes programming more than hardware.
The first two years typically focus on foundational programming courses and general education requirements.
The final two years allow students to specialize through upper-division courses related to their interests.

Advice:

Coursework provides exposure to programming concepts and tools.
Students should build personal projects outside of class to strengthen their programming skills.
Independent practice is important for developing software engineering abilities.

Key Takeaway:

Programming is a central component of the CSE major, but personal projects and self-learning are important for skill development.

Contributor: Mannu Malhotra
Background:

M.S. in Computer Science, National University of Singapore

Opinion:

Many computer science courses are programming-focused.
Assignments often require students to write and test code.
Students may use C or other programming languages depending on the course.

Additional Perspective:

Computer science also includes theoretical subjects.
The balance between programming and theory depends on the courses students choose.

Key Takeaway:

The major contains both practical programming and theoretical computer science.

Contributor: Justin Rising
Background:

Software Engineer and Programmer

Opinion:

Students can expect a significant amount of programming throughout the degree.
Introductory and lower-division computer science courses are usually the most programming-intensive.
Upper-division courses often become more theoretical and mathematical.

Additional Perspective:

Some advanced courses use smaller programming assignments to explore specialized topics.
Programming skills improve substantially through repeated practice and coursework.

Key Takeaway:

Early coursework focuses heavily on coding, while later coursework balances theory, mathematics, and specialized applications.

Key Themes Across the Discussion

Programming Focus:

The CSE major has a strong programming component.
Programming courses form a significant part of the lower-division curriculum.
Students gain experience through coding assignments and projects.

Theory vs. Programming:

Computer science is not exclusively programming.
Upper-division courses often include mathematical, theoretical, and analytical topics.
Students can choose electives that align with their interests.

Advice for Prospective Students:

Build personal projects outside of class.
Practice programming regularly.
Review course descriptions before selecting electives.
Take advantage of programming-focused upper-division courses.

Overall Consensus:

The UC Merced Computer Science and Engineering major is strongly programming-focused, especially during the lower-division sequence. However, students should expect a combination of programming, mathematics, algorithms, systems, and theoretical computer science as they progress through the degree.
