CSE 188: Natural Language Processing

General Course Description

CSE 188 introduces students to topics in:

- Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Language understanding
- Machine learning methods for text
- Modern AI applications involving language

The course is one of the few upper-division electives at UC Merced focused specifically on NLP and modern language AI systems.

Common Student Criticisms

Several students expressed dissatisfaction with the course structure and delivery.

Recurring concerns include:

- Limited engagement during lectures.
- Perception that lectures relied heavily on prepared online materials.
- Feeling that course content was not explained in sufficient depth.
- Difficulty connecting lectures with meaningful learning outcomes.

Representative Feedback:

"I cannot say that I learned anything in this class."

"Don't do it."

One student specifically criticized the course for relying heavily on AI-generated instructional materials and felt that lecture delivery lacked originality and depth.

Advice From Former Students

Students interested in taking CSE 188 may benefit from:

- Having prior machine learning knowledge.
- Reading research papers independently.
- Supplementing lectures with outside resources.
- Treating the course as a research-oriented AI elective.

Overall Student Consensus

The currently available reviews for CSE 188 present mixed feedback.

Positive reviews indicate that students can learn valuable Natural Language Processing concepts and gain exposure to modern AI topics such as Large Language Models.

However, multiple reviews criticize the lecture delivery and overall course structure, with some students reporting that they learned very little from the course.

Because only a small number of reviews are currently available, students should consider these comments as limited anecdotal feedback rather than a definitive assessment of the instructor or course.
