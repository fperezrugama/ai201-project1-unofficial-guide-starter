OVERVIEW

This document summarizes Coursicle information for selected UC Merced Computer Science and Engineering courses. It includes course descriptions, recent professors, class size ranges, student review themes, and difficulty signals. Unrelated website content such as advertisements, TikTok promotions, section links, and repeated navigation text has been removed.

CSE 100: Algorithm Design and Analysis

Course Description:
Introduction to the design and analysis of computer algorithms. Topics include algorithm complexity, divide and conquer, dynamic programming, greedy algorithms, searching and sorting, graphs, data structures, and optimization techniques.

Credits:
- 4 units

Attributes:
- Upper Division Crossroads GE

Recent Professors:
- Shuo Chen
- Hua Huang
- Wesley Maia
- Yi Liu
- Xingjian Ding
- Pengfei Su
- Kianna Ng
- Haide He
- Weijie Lyu
- Rasul Kairgeldin
- Alberto Cerpa
- Kuat Gazizov
- Sravan Jayati
- Liuyao Dai
- Xiaoyi Lu
- Hao Qi

Class Size:
- About 24 to 210 students

Student Review Themes:
- CSE 100 is often described as difficult and important for the CSE major.
- Some reviews mention strict grading and long midterms.
- Automatic grading can be a major factor in student experience.
- Students report that professor choice can significantly affect the course experience.

Representative Student Feedback:
- One student said Miguel Carreira-Perpiñán was strict on grading and that the midterm was very long for the time allowed.
- One student described Alberto Cerpa as sometimes accommodating and kind, but also said his teaching style may not fit all learning preferences.

RAG Notes:
CSE 100 is a foundational upper-division course and is a prerequisite or important preparation for many later CSE courses. Student feedback suggests that the course is challenging regardless of professor, but grading style and exam structure vary by instructor.

CSE 108: Full Stack Web Development

Course Description:
Explores front-end and back-end web development, including web security, scalable architecture, web frameworks, databases, and object-relational mappers.

Recent Professors:
- Ammon Hepworth
- Qhelile Sibanda
- Tri Dang
- Weimin Qiu
- Jingquan Li
- Sravan Jayati
- Dong Lee
- Haolin Liang

Class Size:
- About 28 to 150 students

Student Review Themes:
- CSE 108 is often described as one of the easier upper-division CSE electives.
- Students say it can be useful for practical web development.
- Some students describe the course as self-taught.
- Group projects are an important part of the course.

Representative Student Feedback:
- One student said Professor Ammon Hepworth is one of their favorite CSE professors and that CSE 108 is well taught and useful.
- Another student said the class is easy to pass but criticized it as heavily self-taught and poorly connected between lectures and assignments.

RAG Notes:
CSE 108 is useful for students interested in web development and project-based learning. It may be a lighter elective, but students should expect to learn some tools independently.

Introduces programming, computational thinking, and problem-solving. Students learn programming skills, version control, unit testing, and debugging. The course uses a project-based learning philosophy, with Python as the main language and some exposure to HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and SQL.
