OVERVIEW
The Computer Science and Engineering B.S. degree follows a four-year plan consisting of lower-division preparation, upper-division requirements, technical electives, general education requirements, and a culminating software engineering experience.

APPROACHES TO KNOWLEDGE AND GENERAL EDUCATION RECOMMENDATIONS FOR CSE MAJORS

The courses below are examples of options that satisfy General Education (GE) and Intellectual Experience requirements while also complementing the Computer Science and Engineering major. Students should consult the official catalog for the complete list of approved courses.

AREA A: LIFE AND PHYSICAL SCIENCE

Required for most CSE students:

* PHYS 008: Introductory Physics I for Physical Sciences
* PHYS 009: Introductory Physics II for Physical Sciences

Recommended alternatives and electives:

* COGS 130: Cognitive Neuroscience
* ENVE 010: Environment in Crisis
* ESS 001: Introduction to Earth Systems Science
* PH 110: Environmental Health

Why useful for CSE students:
These courses provide scientific reasoning skills and can complement interests in AI, data science, environmental computing, health technology, and human-centered computing.

AREA B: SOCIAL SCIENCE, HUMANITIES, AND CULTURE

Popular options relevant to CSE students:

* COGS 001: Introduction to Cognitive Science
* ECON 001: Introduction to Economics
* MIST 050: Introduction to Entrepreneurship
* PHIL 102: Epistemology
* POLI 001: Introduction to American Politics
* PSY 001: Introduction to Psychology
* SOC 001: Introduction to Sociology

Additional options:

* GEOG 112: GIS for World Economic History
* HS 112: GIS for World Economic History
* MGMT 118: Women in Executive Leadership

Why useful for CSE students:
These courses help develop understanding of users, organizations, entrepreneurship, decision-making, ethics, and social impacts of technology.

INTELLECTUAL EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENTS

Scientific Method (Recommended for CSE Students)

Courses that align particularly well with computing, AI, and data science:

* CSE 176: Introduction to Machine Learning
* ENGR 080: Statistical Modeling and Data Analysis for Engineers
* MATH 180: Modern Applied Statistics
* MIST 130: Statistical Data Analysis and Optimization in R
* PH 010: Introduction to Biostatistics

These courses are especially valuable because they strengthen quantitative analysis, data interpretation, and machine learning foundations.

Diversity and Identity

Recommended options:

* CCST 060: Introduction to Chicano/a Culture and Experiences
* CRES 001: Introduction to Critical Race and Ethnic Studies
* MGMT 118: Women in Executive Leadership
* POLI 001: Introduction to American Politics
* PSY 124: Health Disparities
* SOC 001: Introduction to Sociology

Global Awareness

Recommended options:

* GEOG 112: GIS for World Economic History
* ENVE 164: Energy Policy
* MGMT 128: Global Markets and Investment Banking
* PH 001: Introduction to Public Health
* POLI 003: Introduction to Comparative Politics

Sustainability

Recommended options:

* ENGR 180: Spatial Analysis and Modeling
* CSE 160: Computer Networks
* ESS 002: Sustainability Science
* ENVE 010: Environment in Crisis
* PH 110: Environmental Health

Particularly useful for students interested in environmental data science, GIS, smart infrastructure, or sustainability applications of computing.

Ethics

Recommended options:

* ENGR 091: Professional Development: People in an Engineered World
* MGMT 123: Business Ethics
* PHIL 002: Introduction to Ethics
* POLI 112: Public Policy: Analysis, Strategy, and Impact
* CHEM 194: Ethics and Communication in Chemistry

These courses help students understand professional responsibility, ethical decision-making, and the societal impact of technology.
